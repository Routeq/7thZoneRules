03

In-Game Rules

Full Rule Content

These are baseline standards. Each incident is reviewed based on context and roleplay impact.

0. Admin Discretion

Consequences: Class B or Class C

When a player's actions or conduct negatively impact another player's roleplay experience and/or undermine the realistic atmosphere we strive to create on 7thzone Roleplay, admins have authority to punish that player for violations not explicitly listed below.

Admins may also punish scenes where poor roleplay is clearly represented and forum reports are unnecessary, if the situation fits this rule.

1. Common Courtesy

Consequences: Any Class (Class A - Class D)

All players should treat others with respect. You are not allowed to break rules because someone else did. You should not disrupt, stall, or void roleplay unless an administrator tells you to.

Examples:

  • Quitting the game to avoid roleplay (combat logging) - automatic 10 day ban.
  • Repeatedly refusing roleplay or avoiding roleplay.
  • Failing to properly roleplay character injuries.
  • Bugs and desync issues are to be roleplayed out.
  • Assets are not to be transferred without approval by Administration.
  • Homophobia, misgendering, and targeted hateful remarks are strongly prohibited.
  • Insulting another player through out-of-character chat (forums, Discord, etc.).
  • Disregarding an administrator decision or ignoring administrator messages.
  • Failure to roleplay a car crash unless caused by desync and voided by admin.
  • Purposely being away from keyboard to avoid roleplay.
  • Properties should be sold at realistic prices.
  • Injuries from violent scenes should be roleplayed until next server restart.
  • Poor escalation can lead to punishment.
  • Report any bug/exploit; exploiting can result in permanent ban.
  • Do not purchase/sell weapons or drugs via in-game Yellow Pages.
  • No weapon refunds if admin jailed for rule breaks with weapon in inventory.

2. Metagaming

Consequences: Class C

Metagaming is using out-of-character information in-character. It can include pulling someone's stream up, being in a Discord call, or plotting out-of-character. Inciting others to metagame also violates this rule.

Examples:

  • Reading out-of-character chat and roleplaying that knowledge in-character.
  • Reading Twitch chat and roleplaying that knowledge in-character.
  • Using Discord/Skype/etc. to communicate in-character while in-game.

3. Powergaming

Consequences: Class B

Powergaming includes performing non-human-like acts, forcing roleplay without fair chance to respond, inventing things that did not happen, or roleplaying impossible script-wise actions for unfair advantage.

Examples:

  • Talking, organizing, or calling out locations while downed.
  • Finding only the fastest way to make money rather than roleplaying mechanics.
  • /me and /do should be used as much as possible to aid scenarios.
  • Driving unrealistically fast repeatedly with no character reason.
  • Purposely stomping to death while abusing health bar mechanics.

4. Deathmatching

Consequences: Class A-C

Killing another person without sufficient roleplay reason is not allowed. Unnecessary provoking also falls under this rule. Terrorism is strictly forbidden.

Vehicle Deathmatching (VDM) means purposely using a vehicle to harm/kill another player. Not every vehicle hit is VDM, but matching cases break the rule.

Revenge killing is also included. If you respawn after execution, your immediate in-character memory of the events leading to death is wiped.

Examples:

  • Attacking another player/group without roleplay story build-up.
  • Using a vehicle to attack with intent to kill/knock out without valid initiation.
  • Respawning and returning to kill someone who previously killed you.
  • Provoking an unarmed fist fight then pulling firearm and killing players.
  • Provoking situations without valid reasons behind it.
  • Deathmatching mentality: unnecessary provoking or force without reason.
  • Not fearing police during a shootout in unrealistic ways.
  • Forcing your way into conflict unrelated to your storyline.

5. Robbing and Scamming

Consequences: Class A or B Depending on Situation

Robbing is taking possessions by force or threat of force. Scamming is offering a good/service and refusing payment or delivery. Both must be done strictly in-character.

  • Players cannot force ATM withdrawals.
  • Escalation is required before robberies; weak escalation is punishable.
  • Players cannot force vehicle sale through scripted commands during robbery.
  • Players cannot force handover of assets not present in area.
  • Players may not scam vehicles or properties.
  • Players may not kill during robbery unless the target escalates it.
  • Scam maximum is $20,000.
  • Players may not rob Police/Fire Department items.
  • Players cannot commit robbery in luxury/high-end/sports cars.
  • Players may rob all cash carried on persona.
  • Looting dead players is forbidden.
  • Groups may not split into distant simultaneous house hits to spread police.
  • Players may not rob someone doing scripted jobs (example: garbage pickup).
  • Use "hands up"/"don't move" only when intent is robbery, not killing.
  • No rob/scam actions inside greenzones.
  • Illegal purchases of drugs/firearms must be cash-only.
  • No robbing player-owned businesses or restaurants.
  • Using OOC knowledge for robberies is metagaming and punishable.
  • Only one robbery every 4 hours.
  • Chain robberies without high-quality roleplay are considered unrealistic.

6. New Life Rule (NLR)

Consequences: Class B

If downed and respawned at hospital, your character forgets immediate events in the current scenario. You may not return to death area for at least 15 minutes or retrieve possessions from involved players.

You may not respawn if advised police or EMS are on the way. You must try to contact medical help while incapacitated.

Creating a new character tied to your deceased character storyline to attack involved people is considered revenge killing and punishable under NLR.

7. Abuse of Game Physics

Consequences: Class A

GTA V physics and movement are hardcoded and cannot be changed via scripting. The following is forbidden to avoid non-roleplay situations.

  • Bunnyhopping to gain speed in situations.
  • Spam punching to deny reaction opportunity.
  • Combat rolling during shootouts.
  • Olympic swimming without roleplay exhaustion.
  • Abuse of jobs, such as spawning job vehicle to evade police.
  • Abusing emote cancellation to avoid falls or enable exploits.
  • Server bugs, desync, and lag must be roleplayed at all times.

8. Third Party Modifications

Consequences: Class D

Any game modification/software giving advantage over others is forbidden. Client-side modding is only allowed for aesthetics and may never alter gameplay function. Third-person FOV modifications are forbidden. First-person FOV is allowed.

This includes mods, trainers, crosshairs, tracers, flawless widescreen (3rd person), unlimited stamina, abnormal aspect ratio (must be 16:9), and audio/visual settings that create unfair advantages.

9. Safezones

Consequences: Class B

A no-crime zone is an area where players may not commit crimes. If the attacked player flees to a safezone, roleplay may continue according to this policy.

Spawn locations and protected areas include:

  • Interiors of City Hall, government offices, law enforcement offices, hospitals, dealerships, banks, DMV, and country club.
  • JFK International Airport.
  • Manhattan Mall interior and parking lot.
  • Parking garages and impound lots.
  • Clothing stores, tattoo shops, barber shops (interior only).
  • Player-owned businesses and connected sidewalks/parking when open.
  • Server events excluding block parties and house parties.
  • Movie theaters and bowling alleys (interior only).

10. Not Valuing Life (NVL)

Consequences: Class B

All players must value their character's life at all times.

  • Treat your character as you would treat yourself.
  • If held at gunpoint, follow instructions.
  • In hostage scenarios, value hostage safety.

Examples of when you are not in direct danger:

  • You are in a vehicle and the engine is not stalled.
  • You have a firearm drawn and aimed before the attacker acts.
  • Attacker view is obstructed or they turn their back.

11. Illegal Activities

Consequences: Class B
  • Criminal activities are limited to 4 players max at a time.
  • Dice gambling is limited to $20,000 max per roll and cash-only.
  • Civilians with non-legal firearms cannot attack freely and must have strict roleplay escalation.
  • Civilians are on an 8-hour cooldown after attacking and must follow robbery rules.
  • No faction members may be involved in civilian attacks.
  • Civilians may not be involved in official faction attacks.
  • Choosing shooters/grinders over role-players is unacceptable.

12. Multiple Characters in One Gang/Group

Consequences: Class B

Multiple characters from the same player in one gang/group is not allowed. Avoid creating characters with overlapping storylines and shared informational goals.

If your character is CK'd in a faction and you want to remain in that faction, your new character must start as a resident/member and work back up naturally.

New characters cannot be related to previous characters. This rule applies in-character.

13. Corruption (IC)

Consequences: Any Class Depending on Situation
  • Giving out Police/Fire Department weapons or gear is corruption and may result in bans without warning.
  • Taking large amounts of money, weapons, or items and not reporting to staff is prohibited.
  • Setting another player up to be killed as PD/FD member is prohibited.
  • Players may only work under one government career at once (Medical Services or Police Department).
  • Players may not be in a legal and illegal faction at the same time.
  • Applying for legal firearms in order to distribute weapons/ammo is prohibited.

14. Refunds

Policy: No Refunds

There is a strict no refund policy on any donation made toward the server. Donations help keep the server running and maintained for community enjoyment. This includes whitelist applications, instant whitelist, and any in-game assets.

15. Real World Trading

Consequences: Any Class Depending on Situation

Real World Trading is trading or buying in-game items for out-of-game currency. This is strongly prohibited.

Example: Giving someone a vehicle in game and receiving out-of-character payment.

16. Vehicle Price Agreement

Consequences: Class B + Character Wipe

Vehicles sold in server must follow this pricing agreement: a seller may add a maximum of $75,000 to the vehicle's original stock price.

Example: A vehicle priced at $200,000 may be sold for a maximum of $275,000.

17. Obscene Roleplay

Consequences: Class D

Obscene roleplay scenes must be conducted away from other players/areas where players are likely to cross. If any player feels uncomfortable, they may opt out and the scene is voided. All participating parties must consent through local OOC.

What is considered obscene roleplay:

  • Erotic or sexual roleplay.
  • Torture-styled roleplay.
  • Roleplay scenes involving gore.
  • Bodily fluid roleplay.

The following should never be roleplayed:

  • Rape or nonconsensual touching, regardless of agreement.
  • Obscene roleplay involving individuals that are not of age.
